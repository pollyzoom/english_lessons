Why does music make us feel good?
Reading lesson / B1 / Topic: Music

Reading
Introduction

No one knows exactly why music has such a profound effect on our emotions. But thanks to scientific studies, we have some exciting clues.

The expectation theory

The most popular theory among scientists goes back to 1956. Philosopher and composer Leonard Meyer suggested that emotion in music is all about what we expect and whether we get it. Meyer used earlier psychological theories which said that emotions appear when we cannot satisfy some desire. This creates frustration or anger, but if we finally find what we're looking for — whether it's love or a piece of chocolate — the reward feels even sweeter.

This, Meyer proposed, is what music does. It creates sonic patterns that make us unconsciously predict what's coming next. If we're right, the brain gives itself a little reward — a surge of dopamine. The constant dance between expectation and outcome fills the brain with a pleasurable mix of emotions.

Fun fact: Dopamine is the same chemical your brain releases when you eat your favourite food or get a great mark at school!
Brain science

In 2001, neuroscientists Anne Blood and Robert Zatorre at McGill University in Montreal used brain scanning to prove that people listening to pleasurable music activate special brain regions called the limbic and paralimbic areas. These areas are connected to euphoric reward responses — similar to the good feelings we get from delicious food or achieving a goal.

The mystery continues

But why does a sequence of sounds with no obvious survival value do the same thing? The truth is no one knows for sure. However, we now have many clues.

We expect rising melodies to continue to rise — but not forever. We expect pleasing harmonies rather than jarring dissonance — but what sounds nice today may have seemed unpleasant two hundred years ago. We expect rhythms to be regular but are surprised when the jumpy syncopation of rock'n'roll suddenly switches to a completely different beat.

We can all agree: music is all around us, regulating our moods and bringing good feelings to those who listen. It raises our spirits, brings excitement, or calms us down.
